A good bath mat does more than just dress up your floor, it keeps you safe on wet tile, soaks up drips, and adds comfort to your morning routine. But you don’t need to spend a fortune to get those benefits. Budget bath mats can be plenty absorbent and non-slip if you know what to look for. When shopping on a budget, focus on three things: a reliable non-slip backing (rubber or latex works best), machine-washable materials for easy cleaning, and a size that fits your space. Skip the ultra-thin mats that slide around, and look for cotton or cotton-blend options that dry quickly. Memory foam can be affordable too, but tends to take longer to dry.
